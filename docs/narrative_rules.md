# Narrative Rules — SALA193

## 1. Prime directive

Story mode must read like ordinary fiction.

No reader should need geopolitical knowledge to enjoy the scene.

The hidden historical layer exists only as a deep structure for character design and event compression.

## 2. Forbidden in story mode

Avoid explicit terms that reveal the base dimension:

- country;
- nation;
- government;
- diplomacy;
- geopolitics;
- border;
- colony;
- empire;
- GDP;
- sanction;
- treaty;
- military alliance;
- world war;
- United Nations;
- global order.

These terms may appear only if they are natural in the fictional setting and do not expose the mapping.

## 3. Show scenes, not explanations

Bad:

> Samuel is powerful and tries to control João.

Good:

> Samuel paid the bill before João could touch his wallet. He smiled as if it were kindness. João smiled back because the room was watching.

## 4. Human scale compression

Macro-events must become human-scale events.

Examples:

| Macro-pattern | Human-scale scene |
| --- | --- |
| occupation | someone starts sleeping on another person's couch and slowly changes the locks |
| debt dependence | someone pays a bill publicly and later asks for loyalty |
| proxy conflict | two powerful characters encourage weaker friends to fight each other |
| resource extraction | someone keeps borrowing tools and returning them broken |
| cultural influence | everyone starts repeating someone else's phrases without noticing |
| failed state | a character's house becomes a place where nobody knows who is in charge |
| revolution | children lock their father out and argue over who holds the keys |
| cold war | two rivals never touch each other but ruin every room they enter through intermediaries |

## 5. Do not over-symbolize settings

Avoid magical houses, obvious map-spaces, or literalized geopolitics.

A bar, court hallway, bus station, family party, hospital waiting room, school meeting, debt office or neighborhood association can hold enough pressure for major scenes.

The setting must feel real first.

## 6. João's narrative voice

João can be the narrator, but he must not be omniscient.

He should be:

- warm;
- observant;
- humorous under stress;
- prone to noticing people before systems;
- sometimes naive;
- sometimes sharper than others expect;
- unwilling to reduce people to one act;
- uncomfortable with clean endings.

João should often understand the emotion of a scene before understanding its cause.

## 7. Scene construction checklist

Every scene should answer:

1. Who wants what?
2. Who can deny it?
3. What is publicly visible?
4. What is privately intended?
5. What changes by the end?
6. What remains unsaid?
7. What will someone remember incorrectly later?

## 8. Dialogue rules

Dialogue must carry power.

Characters should rarely say exactly what they mean.

Examples:

- "Deixa que eu pago" may mean: I own you now.
- "Você que sabe" may mean: choose wrong and I will remember.
- "Estou tentando ajudar" may mean: obey me.
- "Não foi nada" may mean: I will never forgive this.
- "A gente conversa depois" may mean: this is not over.

## 9. Violence rule

Violence should have memory.

A beating, killing, disappearance or humiliation cannot be treated as plot decoration. It must alter posture, rumor, fear, debt, loyalty and future decisions.

## 10. Comedy rule

Comedy is allowed and desirable.

But comedy must not cancel consequence.

João may joke during a disaster. The disaster remains.

## 11. Historical DNA rule

Character sheets may contain historical DNA, but scenes must contain behavior.

Bad hidden-to-visible conversion:

> Ivan acts aggressively because of historical invasions.

Good conversion:

> Ivan always sits facing the door. When someone stands behind him, he stops speaking.

## 12. Reader experience

The ideal reader reaction is:

1. I like these characters.
2. Something feels deep under the surface.
3. These relationships seem bigger than the scene.
4. I want to know what happens next.

The reader does not need to identify the hidden mapping.

## 13. Analysis mode separation

Analysis mode may expose:

- hidden country inspiration;
- historical pattern;
- compression logic;
- possible future arcs;
- mapping weaknesses.

Analysis mode must never leak into story mode output.
